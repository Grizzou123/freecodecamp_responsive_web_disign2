Sir Nicholas Winton

Winton assisted in the rescue of 669 children, most of them Jewish, from Czechoslovakia on the eve of World War II.

Here's a time line of Sir Nicholas Winton:

  • 1909 - Born in London, England

  • 1933 - Join the British Committee for Refugees from Czechoslovakia

  • 1939 - organizes the convoy of children in planes and trains to England and Sweden

  • 1988 - For fifty years, the surviving children were unsure of whom they owed their rescue, and during this half-century, his heroic act went unnoticed. His story was not known until the day his wife Greta discovered a leather towel in an attic containing lists of children and letters from their parents.

  • 2002 - Winton received many tributes including that of Queen Elizabeth II who knighted him for service to humanity

  • 2015 - Died July 2015 (aged 106) Slough, Berkshire, England
